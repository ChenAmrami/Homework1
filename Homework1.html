What is it?

Acaí is a 1-inch round berry that comes from the acai palm tree, which is native to rainforests of Central and South America. The berries have a deep purple skin, yellow flesh, and a large-sized seed inside. They're fairly similar to grapes in their size and structure—the seed takes up about 80 percent of the fruit itself. Acaí berries have a slightly sweet and earthy flavor that's often compared to blueberries paired with very dark chocolate.

History

The açaí berry began to make its way to the masses of Brazil in the 1970s as local migrations of indigenous people prompted the fruit to gain popularity further and further away from the Amazon River delta. But at this point, the fruit was still being eaten in traditional forms. So where did the smoothie bowl originate? For that, we have legendary coach and founder of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Carlos Gracie, to thank. After realizing the tremendous health benefits of the fruit (and low sugar content), Gracie included an açaí and banana blend as a staple in his recommended nutrition plan (The Gracie Diet) for his athletes.

Health Benefits

May Improve Heart Health: Açai is high in nutrients and plant compounds that may benefit heart health in several ways. May Benefit People with Prostate Cancer: Açaí is a concentrated source of plant compounds like anthocyanins that are known for their anticancer properties.3 Although more research is needed, some studies show açaí berries may be helpful for people with prostate cancer.

Acai bowl recipe

Ingredients: 1/2 cup apple juice, 1 large banana, 1 cup frozen berries, 1 (7 ounce) container plain Greek yogurt, 3 1/2 ounces (1 packet) frozen açaí berry purée broken into pieces (see note 1), Assorted toppings such as: fresh fruit, nuts, seeds, shredded coconut, granola, etc.
Instructions: In a high-speed blender, add apple juice, banana, frozen berries, yogurt, and açaí puree. Blend until thoroughly combined and smooth. Divide between 2 deep bowls (1 ½ cups smoothie each). Arrange desired toppings over smoothie and serve.
